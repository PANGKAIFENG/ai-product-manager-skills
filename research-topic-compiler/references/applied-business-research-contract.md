# Applied Business Research Contract

Use this reference when the research must support a real business decision rather than only topic understanding.

## When to read

Read this contract when the user wants research that will influence:

- product strategy, packaging, or go-to-market
- commercialization or business-model choices
- workflow / platform design
- enterprise adoption, implementation, or customer delivery
- feature gating, capability placement, or access policy
- any decision that will be challenged by product, sales, CS, engineering, or management

## Required research posture

- Treat the run as a high-stakes applied decision.
- Default to `L4` unless the user explicitly asks for a lighter pass.
- Separate explanation from decision support.
- Optimize for transfer, not for one-off examples.

## Evidence coverage

Collect evidence across these archetypes when possible:

| Archetype | What it contributes |
| --- | --- |
| Primary facts | Official docs, product pages, pricing pages, changelogs, system docs, standards |
| Case patterns | Comparable products, public adoption examples, implementation notes |
| Frameworks / methods | Packaging heuristics, pricing patterns, decision frameworks, operating models |
| Counterexamples | Failure modes, adoption friction, overfit cases, rejected patterns |
| Context mapping | How the source user, workflow, assets, and constraints compare to the user's situation |

## Transfer reasoning

For every important source or case, answer:

1. What problem did it actually solve?
2. What pattern is reusable here?
3. What boundary makes it risky to copy?
4. What does this imply for the user's decision?

## Decision artifact requirements

The final output should usually include:

- decision context: who decides, what decision, why now, what is out of scope
- judgment matrix: how capabilities, features, or workflows are placed into tiers, add-ons, connectors, or services
- staged recommendation: what belongs in baseline access, premium access, gated trial, add-on, connector, enterprise, or implementation
- risks and counterexamples: what could break if the pattern is copied too early or too literally
- open validations: which assumptions still need market or customer proof

## Anti-patterns

- Summarizing the topic without saying what transfers
- Basing the answer on one flagship case
- Letting cost analysis replace value or adoption analysis
- Treating "can connect" as equivalent to "should charge"
- Writing a generic list of sources with no decision consequence
- Making the output about this one example instead of the broader decision pattern

## Completion bar

A useful applied-business research run should leave behind:

- a clear business judgment
- a layered model or placement logic
- a comparison table or matrix
- a staged path for adoption or monetization
- a short list of validation gaps
